General Information on Watercolour Paint

Introduction to Watercolours

Watercolor paint is a water-soluble paint medium. It is composed of gum arabic and a pigment, which can be man-made or natural. Watercolors are usually semi-translucent, as they are most often used with water. Watercolour is applied using a watered brush on specialized paper. Watercolour paper is usually thick, allowing it to absorb the water without bending or crumpling. Compared to oil and acrylic paints, the pigment of watercolour is very light, but is used mostly by layering coats of paint at a time. Because watercolour is painted on paper, it decays much faster than other mediums on canvas.
